Design Philosophy

Welcome to FM8/R, a professional groovebox engineered by Another Machines for producers to create their own unique loops for any DAW. While it lives in a virtual cloud, its heart is modular, offering deep sound sculpting capabilities inspired by hardware workflows. We are constantly in development, with exciting new features planned for future updates.

This is not just a tool; it's an instrument for sonic exploration.

The Sound Engines

FM8/R features 8 distinct sound engines, each designed for a specific sonic purpose. Understanding them is key to mastering the machine.

  • KICK: A powerful, punchy drum synth for crafting everything from tight clicks to booming sub-basses.
  • HAT: A versatile noise and resonator engine for creating a wide range of hi-hats, cymbals, and metallic percussion.
  • ARCANE: A dual-oscillator synth with phase modulation, sync, and wavefolding. Perfect for complex digital tones, pads, and aggressive leads.
  • RUIN: A distortion and feedback-focused engine for destructive, noisy, and chaotic basslines and textures.
  • ARTIFICE: A sophisticated synth voice with dual multi-mode filters, FM, and noise mixing. Ideal for intricate sound design, evolving pads, and complex percussive hits.
  • SHIFT: A wavetable oscillator with unique 'bend' and 'twist' parameters for creating shifting, glassy, and formant-like sounds.
  • RESON: A physical modeling engine that simulates the sound of struck objects. Excellent for realistic (and unrealistic) bells, plucks, and resonant percussion.
  • ALLOY: A 2-operator FM synth designed for creating classic metallic, bell-like, and electric piano sounds with a sharp, percussive character.

The Sequencer

The main grid is where you create patterns. Click a step to activate it. The selected track's instrument will play on that step. You can change pattern length, randomize, and create complex rhythms using Euclidean mode.

P-Locks (Parameter Locks): Enable P-LOCK mode to edit parameters for individual steps. Select a step, then adjust any knob in the Instrument Editor or Mixer. This locks the new value to that specific step, creating dynamic, evolving patterns.

Trig Conditions: When in P-LOCK mode, select a step to access its Trigger Condition. This allows you to set rules for when a step will play (e.g., '50%' probability, or only on the '2nd' time through the pattern), adding immense variation.

Euclidean Rhythms: Activate Euclidean mode from the sequencer toolbar to generate complex, musical rhythms automatically. Adjust Pulses, Steps, and Rotation to explore a world of polyrhythms.

Instrument Editor

Select a track to see its sound engine parameters here. Each engine has a unique set of controls. Experiment to find new sounds! Detailed descriptions of each engine's core purpose can be found in the "Sound Engines" section above.

Presets: Save and load instrument sounds using the preset manager at the top. This is a great way to build your personal sound library and speed up your workflow.

Automation: Activate the REC AUTOM button to record knob movements. Any parameter you tweak while recording will be captured as automation for the selected track, adding life and movement to your sounds.

Mixer & Piano Roll

The bottom center panel serves two roles. By default, it's the Mixer, where you can adjust volume, pan, and effect sends for each of the 8 tracks. This is where you balance your overall sound.

For melodic tracks, you can toggle to the Piano Roll view using the keyboard icon in the sequencer toolbar. This provides a traditional piano-style interface for composing melodies, chords, and basslines with precise control over note pitch and length.

Master Effects

The right-hand panel contains the master effects rack, which processes the entire mix. Use these to add polish, space, and character to your sound.

  • Master Filter: A global LP/HP filter for broad tonal shaping and buildups.
  • Character: Adds harmonic complexity through four modes: Saturate, Overdrive, Bitcrush, and Fold.
  • Delay: A tempo-synced or free-running delay for creating echoes and rhythmic complexity.
  • Reverb: Adds space and depth to your mix, from small rooms to vast caverns.
  • Drive: A master distortion unit for adding warmth, grit, or aggressive overdrive to the entire mix.
  • Master Compressor: The final "glue" for your track. It helps to control dynamics and increase perceived loudness. Can be sidechained from any track.

Song Mode

Switch from PATTERN to SONG mode to arrange your patterns into a full track. In this view, you can place clips of your patterns onto a timeline for each track.

Click ADD PATTERN, select a pattern number, and click on the timeline to place a clip. You can then move, resize, and duplicate clips to build your song structure. Use the Loop control at the top of the timeline to define a region for playback or export.

Saving & Loading

You can manage both full projects and individual instrument sounds.

  • Project Library: Access via the project name display in the top header. Here you can save, load, rename, and delete entire projects (all 8 tracks, patterns, and settings).
  • Instrument Presets: Found at the top of the Instrument Editor. This allows you to save and load the sound of a single track, making it easy to reuse your favorite kick, bass, or lead sounds across different projects.

Export & Share

When you're ready to take your loops out of FM8/R, use the EXPORT button. You have several options:

  • Master Output: Renders a single stereo WAV file of your full mix.
  • Wet Stems: Exports each audible track as a separate WAV file, including the master send effects (Reverb, Delay, Drive).
  • Dry Stems: Exports each track as a pure, clean WAV file with no master effects, perfect for mixing in your DAW.

The SHARE JAM button opens a powerful sharing suite. You can record a video of your current loop—complete with a dynamic visualizer—and download it as a .webm file for social media. You can also generate a unique link with your compressed project data for others to listen to instantly in their browser.

MIDI Control

FM8/R has a powerful MIDI Learn system. Go to Settings > MIDI Mapping and click the MIDI LEARN button.

While in learn mode, first click any parameter (knob, button, etc.) on the screen, then move a physical control on your MIDI controller. The mapping will be created automatically. You can also send MIDI out from a MIDI track to control external hardware or software.

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Spacebar: Play / Pause
  • Ctrl/Cmd + C (on step): Copy Step
  • Ctrl/Cmd + V (on step): Paste Step
  • Ctrl/Cmd + Shift + C: Copy Pattern
  • Ctrl/Cmd + Shift + V: Paste Pattern

System Requirements & Disclaimer

For the best experience, please ensure your system meets the following recommendations.

  • Browser: The latest version of Google Chrome is strongly recommended for optimal performance and full compatibility with the Web Audio and Web MIDI APIs. Other modern browsers (Firefox, Edge) may work but are not officially supported.
  • CPU: Intel Core i5 (or equivalent) 2.5GHz or faster. Real-time audio synthesis is CPU-intensive.
  • RAM: 8GB minimum, 16GB or more recommended for complex projects.
  • Operating System: A modern desktop OS such as Windows 10+, macOS 11+, or a recent Linux distribution.

Disclaimer: FM8/R is an experimental instrument under active development. While we strive for stability, you may encounter occasional bugs or performance issues. We appreciate your understanding and feedback as we continue to improve the experience.
